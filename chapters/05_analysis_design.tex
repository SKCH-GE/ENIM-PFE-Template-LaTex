\section{Data Flow and Control Flow}
Explain how information moves through the system and how decisions or operations are triggered.

Avoid mixing normal flows, error flows, and exceptional cases in one unreadable diagram. Split diagrams when needed.

\section{Design Decisions}
Record the important choices made during design, especially those that affect maintainability, performance, correctness, cost, or user experience.

Avoid documenting trivial choices. Focus on decisions a future maintainer or evaluator would need to understand.

\section{Design Diagrams}
Include UML diagrams, database schemas, sequence diagrams, deployment diagrams, wireframes, or other views that clarify the solution.

Use each diagram in the text: introduce it, explain what it shows, and refer to the design decision it supports.

\section{Conclusion}
Conclude by summarizing the selected design and preparing the transition to implementation.
