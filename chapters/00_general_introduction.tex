\label{chap:general-introduction}

Use the general introduction to lead the reader from the broad domain to the precise report problem. Start with the real context, explain the gap or need, then state the project objective and the structure of the report.

Say what problem exists, who is affected by it, why it matters now, and what contribution your work intends to make. Keep the opening connected to the rest of the report, especially the requirements, methodology, and validation chapters.

Avoid beginning with vague claims such as ``technology is everywhere'' unless they quickly become specific. Do not present results before the validation chapter, do not overpromise the scope, and do not include company history that belongs in the project-context chapter.

The final paragraph should announce the organization of the report in the same order as the chapters. Keep this roadmap short and factual.
